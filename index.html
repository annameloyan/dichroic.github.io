Cyber Arts Camp

Learn cutting edge technology and sustainability from the leading minds in their fields.

The Martians are in the dark and need YOUR help. Beings from across the universe will teleport to your computer and teach you the skills necessary to build a solar-powered kinetic sculpture to illuminate the night sky and free the Martians.

Lessons

  • Learn

    We'll take you on a journey to design, construct, and illuminate your own kinetic light sculpture. No prior coding experience necessary. All materials are included in your space kit.

  • Arduino

    Become the master of your universe with the Arduino, a cheap, programmable, open-source microcontroller which makes it easy to control motors, lights, electronics, and many other things.

  • LEDs

    Go from 0 to 255 with RGB. Learn how to address individual LEDs, additive versus subtractive mediums, and explore how rainbows are created through light refraction.

  • Solar Power

    Where we're going, we don't need... outlets. Learn how to harness the power of the sun with our solar power kits.

  • Art

    Cyber Arts Camp coaches are a diverse group of creative practitioners working at the intersection of art and technology. Students will engage with these topics through creative problem solving.

  • Sculpture

    Let's travel to the third dimension with the help of Buckminster Fuller while exploring geometric and architectural principles.

  • Modularity

    Component parts, a whole comprised of indivudal pieces, reconfiguration and variation

  • Sustain

    Sustainability starts with self-reliance. Learn how to build, understand, maintain, and modify each of the components of your sculpture.

  • Create

    Synthesize these concepts into a unique light sculpture

FAQ

  • When does the camp run?

    Cyber Arts Camp starts June 23rd and will run for two weeks, Monday through Friday, for 10 total classes.

  • What is a day in cyber arts camp like?

    We start the day with an entertaining and educational video on the topic of the day. We then go through detailed instructions on how to build and work on the next piece in your sculpture. Total time is about 2 to 5 hours a day.

  • Will there be coaches or support?

    Yes! Our have Martian coaches that will help you along the way during studio hours and through online chat.

  • What will we be building?

    At the end of the camp, each student will have designed, built, soldered, and programmed their own unique light sculpture.

  • What results can a student expect?

    Students will walk away with a thorough understanding of physical computing, electronics, and a feeling of creative possibility and empowerment.

  • Are the materials provided?

    Yes, we will be sending a Martian kit that will have all the materials needed including LED lights, Arduino, Soldering Kit, Building Materials, a $100 dollar value!

  • Is supervision advised?

    Adult supervision is advised during our soldering and solar power tutorials (2 days), as we'll be working with a soldering iron and electric wires.

  • Will computers be provided?

    No, students must have their own computer or laptop with internet access.

We're the Martians

We're called Mars Radio because of our focus on technology and self sustained living. We are a cooperative of artists, technologists, and futurists that believe we can harness our collective knowledge to produce amazing results that can improve our world. We want to share our knowledge with you through a fun interactive program.
